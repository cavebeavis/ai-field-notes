# Title

## Abstract

State the problem, central claim, approach, and conclusion without overselling certainty.

## Problem

Define the concrete problem and its boundaries.

## Thesis

State the argument in falsifiable or challengeable terms where possible.

## Context

Explain the relevant technical, economic, historical, or organizational background.

## Evidence

Present sources, observations, measurements, experiments, and examples. Distinguish direct evidence from interpretation.

## Analysis

Connect the evidence to the thesis. Expose assumptions and intermediate reasoning that readers would otherwise have to guess.

## Counterarguments and alternatives

Present the strongest competing explanations or designs, not weak substitutes.

## Implications

Describe what changes if the thesis is correct, and who or what is affected.

## Limitations

State where the argument does not apply and what remains unknown.

## Recommendations

List concrete actions only when the evidence supports them.

## Conclusion

Restate the durable result without merely repeating the introduction.

## References

Use stable citations and record access dates for changing web sources.
